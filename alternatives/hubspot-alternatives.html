10 Best HubSpot Alternatives in 2026: An Honest Comparison

January 2026 16 min read
TL;DR

The best HubSpot alternative for sales-focused teams is Pipedrive—visual pipeline simplicity without marketing bloat. For comprehensive features at lower cost, choose Zoho CRM. For email marketing automation, choose ActiveCampaign. For enterprise needs, Salesforce offers maximum customization. For agencies, GoHighLevel provides unlimited clients at flat-rate pricing. Your choice depends on whether you need marketing automation or can work with CRM-only solutions.

HubSpot revolutionized the CRM market with its genuinely useful free tier and marketing-first approach. But it's not the right fit for everyone. Many teams find that HubSpot's pricing scales aggressively with contacts, the Marketing Hub costs more than they can justify, and they're paying for features they don't use.

The good news: there are excellent alternatives for every use case. Some offer simpler sales-focused experiences, others beat HubSpot on price, and a few provide superior marketing automation at specific functions. This guide compares 10 real options based on actual use cases—not feature lists.

We work with sales teams across different CRMs daily (FlightSuite integrates with HubSpot, GoHighLevel, and more), so we understand what actually matters in real workflows.

Why Companies Look for HubSpot Alternatives

Before exploring alternatives, let's be clear about the real reasons teams consider switching:

  • Contact-based pricing: HubSpot charges based on contacts, not users. A database of 50,000 contacts costs significantly more than 5,000—even if you have the same team size.
  • Marketing Hub costs: The free CRM is excellent, but Marketing Hub Professional starts at $800/month. That's a steep jump for many small businesses.
  • Feature lock-in: Essential features like A/B testing, custom reporting, and predictive lead scoring require expensive tier upgrades.
  • Overkill for sales-only teams: If you don't need marketing automation, you're paying for platform overhead you won't use.
  • Complexity: HubSpot's all-in-one approach means more to configure and maintain than simpler alternatives.

If these concerns resonate, the alternatives below address specific pain points without requiring you to abandon everything that made HubSpot attractive.

The 10 Best HubSpot Alternatives

1. Pipedrive

Best for Sales Simplicity
Price: $14-$99/user/mo Best for: Sales teams who don't need marketing automation

Pipedrive is the anti-HubSpot in the best way. Where HubSpot tries to be everything, Pipedrive focuses relentlessly on sales pipeline management. The visual Kanban-style interface is immediately intuitive—most teams are productive within hours, not weeks.

The key advantage: pricing is per user, not per contact. Your database can grow indefinitely without affecting your bill. For sales teams that don't need marketing automation, Pipedrive delivers better value and simpler operations.

Pros

  • Visual pipeline is beautifully simple
  • User-based pricing (not contacts)
  • Fast setup, minimal training
  • Excellent mobile app
  • Strong automation at all tiers

Cons

  • No real marketing automation
  • Limited reporting vs. HubSpot
  • Email marketing is basic
  • No free tier

2. Zoho CRM

Best Value for Money
Price: Free / $14-$52/user/mo Best for: Budget-conscious teams wanting comprehensive features

Zoho CRM offers perhaps the best feature-to-price ratio in the market. It includes AI lead scoring (Zia), workflow automation, and multichannel communication at prices that significantly undercut HubSpot's paid tiers.

The real power emerges with Zoho One ($45/user/month for 45+ apps)—a full business suite that rivals HubSpot's ecosystem at a fraction of the cost. The trade-off is polish; Zoho's interface isn't as refined, and setup takes more effort.

Pros

  • Aggressive pricing
  • AI features included (Zia)
  • Zoho One ecosystem (45+ apps)
  • Free tier for up to 3 users
  • Strong customization options

Cons

  • Less polished interface
  • Steeper learning curve
  • Support can be inconsistent
  • Integration ecosystem smaller

3. ActiveCampaign

Best Email Marketing
Price: $29-$149/mo (based on contacts) Best for: Teams prioritizing email automation over CRM

If email marketing is your primary need and CRM is secondary, ActiveCampaign delivers superior automation at lower prices than HubSpot Marketing Hub. The visual automation builder is genuinely powerful, and email deliverability rates are industry-leading.

ActiveCampaign's CRM is capable but simpler than HubSpot's. For marketing-first teams willing to trade some CRM depth for better email features, this is the smarter investment.

Pros

  • Superior email automation
  • Excellent deliverability
  • Visual automation builder
  • Lower cost than HubSpot Marketing
  • Great for complex sequences

Cons

  • CRM is secondary feature
  • Contact-based pricing
  • Interface can be overwhelming
  • Landing page builder is basic

4. Salesforce

Enterprise Power
Price: $25-$330/user/mo Best for: Enterprises needing maximum customization

Salesforce is the opposite direction from most HubSpot alternatives—more complex, not less. But if HubSpot's limitations are frustrating you (reporting depth, custom objects, workflow complexity), Salesforce offers virtually unlimited customization.

This isn't for teams seeking simplicity. Salesforce requires dedicated admin resources and significant implementation investment. But for enterprises that have outgrown HubSpot's capabilities, it's the industry standard for good reasons.

Pros

  • Unlimited customization
  • Massive integration ecosystem
  • Enterprise-grade reporting
  • Industry-specific solutions
  • AppExchange marketplace

Cons

  • Requires dedicated admin
  • Expensive total cost of ownership
  • Complex implementation
  • Marketing Cloud is separate cost

5. Freshsales (Freshworks CRM)

Price: Free / $15-$69/user/mo Best for: SMBs wanting AI features without enterprise pricing

Freshsales delivers HubSpot-like ease of use with AI capabilities (Freddy AI) at significantly lower prices. Lead scoring, deal insights, and workflow automation feel modern and actually work well for teams without technical resources.

As part of the Freshworks suite, it integrates well with Freshdesk (support) and Freshmarketer (marketing). The free tier is functional for very small teams, making it a genuine HubSpot Free alternative.

Pros

  • AI lead scoring (Freddy)
  • Clean, modern interface
  • Built-in phone & email
  • Functional free tier
  • Lower price than HubSpot

Cons

  • Marketing automation limited
  • Smaller integration ecosystem
  • Less mature than HubSpot
  • Reporting could be deeper

6. GoHighLevel

Price: $97-$497/mo (flat rate) Best for: Marketing agencies managing multiple clients

GoHighLevel is designed for agencies that manage multiple client accounts. Unlike HubSpot (or any traditional CRM), GHL charges a flat monthly rate regardless of how many clients or contacts you manage. For agencies, this changes the economics entirely.

The platform includes CRM, funnel builder, email marketing, SMS, and more—everything an agency needs to deliver client work. White-labeling lets you rebrand it as your own. This isn't for single businesses, but for agencies, it's a category of its own.

Pros

  • Flat-rate pricing (unlimited clients)
  • Full white-labeling
  • Funnel builder included
  • SMS marketing native
  • SaaS mode for reselling

Cons

  • Steeper learning curve
  • Interface less polished
  • Limited third-party integrations
  • Support via chat/community

7. Monday Sales CRM

Price: $12-$24/user/mo Best for: Teams already using Monday.com

Monday Sales CRM brings the flexibility of Monday.com's Work OS to sales processes. It's highly visual, infinitely customizable without code, and familiar to anyone who's used Monday for project management.

This works best for teams that want CRM tightly integrated with project tracking, or those consolidating tools onto the Monday platform. It's more flexible than traditional CRMs but lacks dedicated marketing automation.

Pros

  • Highly visual and customizable
  • No-code automation
  • Integrates with Monday Work OS
  • Flexible for unique workflows
  • Affordable per-user pricing

Cons

  • No marketing automation
  • Not built specifically for sales
  • Email integration less robust
  • Reporting is basic

8. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Price: Free / $25-$65/mo Best for: Email-first teams needing affordable multichannel

Brevo offers email marketing, SMS, chat, and CRM in one platform at prices that significantly undercut HubSpot. The pricing model is based on emails sent, not contacts stored—meaning you can have unlimited contacts on the free tier.

The CRM functionality is simpler than HubSpot, but for teams where email and SMS marketing are primary and CRM is secondary, Brevo delivers better value. The free tier is genuinely useful (300 emails/day limit).

Pros

  • Email-based pricing (not contacts)
  • Unlimited contacts on free tier
  • Email + SMS + Chat unified
  • Good automation features
  • WhatsApp integration

Cons

  • CRM is secondary feature
  • Interface less intuitive
  • Limited integrations
  • Advanced features need upgrades

9. Close

Price: $49-$139/user/mo Best for: High-volume inside sales teams

Close is built for inside sales teams that live on the phone. It includes built-in VoIP calling, SMS, and email—no integrations needed. Power dialers, predictive dialers, and call recording are native features, not add-ons.

If your team makes 50+ calls per day, Close eliminates the friction of switching between CRM and dialer. It's more expensive than some alternatives but potentially replaces multiple tools.

Pros

  • Built-in calling & SMS
  • Power dialer included
  • Call recording & coaching
  • Designed for velocity sales
  • Strong automation

Cons

  • Higher price point
  • Overkill for low-volume teams
  • No marketing automation
  • Not for field sales

10. Keap (formerly Infusionsoft)

Price: $159-$229/mo Best for: Small businesses needing sales + marketing automation

Keap is the closest direct HubSpot competitor for small businesses—it combines CRM with marketing automation in a single platform. If you need both and find HubSpot too complex or expensive, Keap offers a middle ground.

The automation builder is powerful but has a learning curve. Keap shines for service-based businesses (coaches, consultants, agencies) who need appointment scheduling, invoicing, and follow-up sequences integrated with CRM.

Pros

  • Sales + marketing unified
  • Invoicing built-in
  • Appointment scheduling
  • Powerful automation
  • Good for service businesses

Cons

  • Significant learning curve
  • Can feel dated at times
  • Pricing is per-contact
  • Less intuitive than HubSpot

How to Choose the Right Alternative

Rather than comparing features endlessly, focus on these decision criteria:

1. What's Your Primary Use Case?

  • Sales pipeline only: Pipedrive or Freshsales
  • Email marketing focus: ActiveCampaign or Brevo
  • Marketing + Sales combined: Zoho CRM or Keap
  • Agency/multi-client: GoHighLevel
  • Enterprise complexity: Salesforce

2. What's Driving the Switch?

  • Contact pricing too high: Pipedrive, Zoho, Monday (user-based pricing)
  • Marketing Hub too expensive: ActiveCampaign, Brevo (better email value)
  • Too complex: Pipedrive, Monday Sales CRM (simpler)
  • Need more features: Salesforce, Zoho One (deeper capability)

3. What's Your Budget Reality?

  • Free or minimal: Zoho Free, Freshsales Free, Brevo Free
  • Under $20/user: Pipedrive, Monday Sales CRM, Zoho
  • $50-100/user: Freshsales Growth, Close, HubSpot Starter
  • Flat rate (agencies): GoHighLevel ($97-497/month total)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best alternative to HubSpot?
The best HubSpot alternative depends on your needs. Pipedrive is best for sales-focused teams wanting visual pipeline simplicity. Zoho CRM is best for budget-conscious businesses needing comprehensive features. ActiveCampaign is best for email marketing and automation focus. Salesforce is best for enterprises needing maximum customization. For most small-to-midsize businesses, Pipedrive or Zoho offer the best value with less complexity.
Why do companies switch from HubSpot?
Companies switch from HubSpot primarily due to pricing that scales aggressively with contacts (not users), marketing hub costs that exceed budget, feature lock-in requiring expensive tier upgrades, and complexity for teams that only need CRM basics. The free CRM is excellent, but paid tiers can become expensive quickly, especially the Marketing Hub which many alternatives undercut significantly.
Is Pipedrive cheaper than HubSpot?
Yes, Pipedrive is typically 30-50% cheaper than HubSpot for sales-only teams. Pipedrive charges per user ($14-99/user/month) regardless of contact database size. HubSpot's pricing increases based on contacts—a team with 50,000 contacts pays significantly more than a team with 2,000. However, HubSpot includes marketing automation that Pipedrive lacks, so the comparison depends on your needs.
Can I export my data from HubSpot?
Yes, HubSpot allows full data export across all tiers including the free CRM. You can export contacts, companies, deals, and activities in CSV format. Most alternatives have import tools that accept HubSpot exports directly. The main migration challenge is recreating workflows and automations, not moving the data itself.
What CRM has better email marketing than HubSpot?
ActiveCampaign and Mailchimp offer more advanced email marketing at lower price points than HubSpot Marketing Hub. ActiveCampaign excels at complex automation sequences and delivers better deliverability rates. Mailchimp provides easier template design and better analytics. However, neither integrates CRM and marketing as seamlessly as HubSpot—you may need multiple tools.
