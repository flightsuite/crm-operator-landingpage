10 Best Salesforce Alternatives in 2026: An Honest Comparison

January 2026 18 min read
TL;DR

The best Salesforce alternative for most businesses is HubSpot—it offers comparable features with lower complexity and 40-60% lower total cost. For sales-focused simplicity, choose Pipedrive. For budget constraints, choose Zoho CRM. For Microsoft-heavy environments, choose Dynamics 365. For agencies, choose GoHighLevel. The right choice depends on your team size, technical resources, and primary use case.

Salesforce is the industry standard for enterprise CRM, but it's not the right choice for everyone. Many companies find that Salesforce is over-engineered for their needs, requires expensive consultants to maintain, and suffers from low user adoption due to its complexity.

The good news: the CRM market has matured significantly. Several alternatives now offer 80-90% of Salesforce's functionality at a fraction of the cost and complexity. This guide compares the 10 best options based on real usage—not vendor marketing.

We work with sales teams using various CRMs (FlightSuite integrates with HubSpot, GoHighLevel, and more), so we understand what actually matters in day-to-day operations.

Why Companies Leave Salesforce

Before diving into alternatives, let's be honest about why you might be looking. Common reasons include:

  • Total cost of ownership: License fees are just the beginning. Implementation, customization, training, and ongoing admin costs often add 2-5x the sticker price.
  • Complexity: Salesforce's flexibility is a double-edged sword. Simple changes often require admin involvement or consultant hours.
  • Low adoption: The steep learning curve leads to resistance. When reps don't use the CRM, data quality suffers.
  • Over-engineering: Enterprise features you'll never use still add to the cognitive load and interface clutter.
  • Admin dependency: Unlike simpler CRMs, Salesforce often requires a dedicated admin role.

If these problems sound familiar, the alternatives below may deliver better ROI through higher adoption and lower overhead.

The 10 Best Salesforce Alternatives

1. HubSpot CRM

Best Overall Alternative
Price: Free / $15-$150/user/mo Best for: SMBs wanting marketing + sales alignment

HubSpot is the most direct Salesforce competitor and the best choice for most companies leaving Salesforce. It offers a genuinely useful free tier, an intuitive interface that requires minimal training, and native marketing automation that Salesforce charges extra for.

The key differentiator: HubSpot's total cost of ownership is typically 40-60% lower than Salesforce. You don't need dedicated admins, implementation is weeks instead of months, and marketing tools are included rather than bolted on.

Pros

  • Free tier is genuinely useful
  • Intuitive, minimal training needed
  • Marketing automation included
  • No dedicated admin required
  • Excellent documentation & support

Cons

  • Less customizable than Salesforce
  • Pricing scales with contacts
  • Some advanced features are new
  • API rate limits on lower tiers

2. Pipedrive

Best for Sales Simplicity
Price: $14-$99/user/mo Best for: Sales teams wanting visual pipeline management

Pipedrive is built by salespeople for salespeople. Its visual, Kanban-style pipeline makes deal tracking intuitive, and it consciously avoids feature bloat. If Salesforce felt overwhelming, Pipedrive will feel like a breath of fresh air.

This is the best choice for teams that only need sales CRM functionality—no marketing automation, no service desk, just pure pipeline management done exceptionally well.

Pros

  • Visual pipeline is intuitive
  • Fast to set up and learn
  • Excellent mobile app
  • Strong automation at all tiers
  • Great value at lower tiers

Cons

  • No marketing automation
  • Limited reporting vs. Salesforce
  • Not ideal for complex enterprises
  • Email marketing is basic

3. Zoho CRM

Best Value for Money
Price: Free / $14-$52/user/mo Best for: Budget-conscious businesses needing full features

Zoho CRM offers perhaps the best feature-to-price ratio in the market. It includes AI-powered lead scoring (Zia), workflow automation, and multichannel communication at prices that undercut most competitors significantly.

The trade-off is polish—Zoho's interface isn't as refined as HubSpot or Pipedrive. But for teams prioritizing features per dollar, Zoho delivers excellent value, especially as part of the broader Zoho One suite.

Pros

  • Aggressive pricing
  • AI features included (Zia)
  • Part of Zoho One ecosystem
  • Strong automation capabilities
  • Free tier for up to 3 users

Cons

  • Interface less polished
  • Learning curve exists
  • Support can be slow
  • Some features feel dated

4. Microsoft Dynamics 365

Best for Microsoft Environments
Price: $65-$162/user/mo Best for: Enterprises already invested in Microsoft

If your company runs on Microsoft 365, Teams, and Azure, Dynamics 365 offers the tightest integration possible. Data flows seamlessly between Outlook, Excel, Power BI, and the CRM without third-party connectors.

Dynamics 365 is a true enterprise platform—it can match Salesforce's customization depth. However, it shares Salesforce's complexity concerns. This is a lateral move for enterprises, not a simplification.

Pros

  • Native Microsoft integration
  • Enterprise-grade customization
  • Power Platform (low-code) access
  • LinkedIn Sales Navigator integration
  • Strong compliance certifications

Cons

  • Similar complexity to Salesforce
  • Requires technical resources
  • Interface can be clunky
  • Implementation takes months

5. Freshsales (Freshworks CRM)

Price: Free / $15-$69/user/mo Best for: SMB sales teams wanting AI features

Freshsales combines an intuitive interface with genuinely useful AI capabilities. Freddy AI scores leads, suggests next actions, and can even draft emails. For smaller teams wanting AI assistance without Salesforce Einstein's complexity, this is compelling.

Part of the Freshworks suite (Freshdesk, Freshmarketer), it integrates well if you adopt multiple products. The free tier is limited but functional for very small teams.

Pros

  • AI lead scoring (Freddy)
  • Clean, modern interface
  • Built-in phone & email
  • Visual sales sequences
  • Good mobile app

Cons

  • Marketing automation limited
  • Fewer integrations than leaders
  • Less mature ecosystem
  • Reporting could be deeper

6. Monday Sales CRM

Price: $12-$24/user/mo Best for: Teams already using Monday.com

Monday Sales CRM brings the flexibility of Monday.com's Work OS to sales. It's highly visual, infinitely customizable (without code), and familiar to anyone who's used Monday for project management.

This works best for teams that want CRM tightly integrated with project tracking, or those who already use Monday.com and want to consolidate tools. It's more flexible than traditional CRMs but less sales-specific than Pipedrive or Freshsales.

Pros

  • Highly visual and customizable
  • No-code automation
  • Integrates with Monday Work OS
  • Flexible for unique workflows
  • Excellent collaboration features

Cons

  • Not built specifically for sales
  • Email integration less robust
  • Reporting is basic
  • Can feel overwhelming initially

7. GoHighLevel

Price: $97-$497/mo (flat rate) Best for: Marketing agencies and resellers

GoHighLevel is designed specifically for agencies that serve multiple clients. Unlike per-user pricing, GHL charges a flat monthly rate regardless of how many clients you manage—making it dramatically cheaper at scale.

The white-label capability lets agencies rebrand the platform entirely. This isn't a Salesforce replacement for most businesses, but for agencies, it's a category of its own. Many agencies generate SaaS revenue by reselling GHL under their own brand.

Pros

  • Flat-rate pricing (unlimited clients)
  • Full white-labeling
  • Built-in funnel builder
  • SMS marketing included
  • SaaS mode for reselling

Cons

  • Steeper learning curve
  • Less polished interface
  • Limited integrations
  • Support via chat/community

8. Copper

Price: $23-$134/user/mo Best for: Google Workspace-centric teams

Copper lives entirely inside Gmail and Google Workspace. There's no separate interface to learn—your CRM appears as a sidebar while you work in Gmail, automatically capturing contacts and conversations.

If your team lives in Google Workspace and hates context-switching, Copper removes friction entirely. The trade-off: it's less powerful than standalone CRMs and won't work for Microsoft-based teams.

Pros

  • Native Gmail integration
  • Automatic data capture
  • Zero context switching
  • Clean, simple interface
  • Google-recommended partner

Cons

  • Requires Google Workspace
  • Less powerful than dedicated CRMs
  • Pricing increases quickly
  • Limited marketing features

9. Close

Price: $49-$139/user/mo Best for: High-volume inside sales teams

Close is built for inside sales teams that make high call volumes. It includes built-in calling (VoIP), SMS, and email—no third-party integrations needed. Power dialers, predictive dialers, and call recording are native features.

If your team makes 50+ calls per day and lives on the phone, Close eliminates the friction of switching between CRM and dialer. It's more expensive than some alternatives but replaces multiple tools.

Pros

  • Built-in calling & SMS
  • Power dialer included
  • Call recording & coaching
  • Designed for velocity sales
  • Strong automation

Cons

  • Higher price point
  • Overkill for low-volume teams
  • Limited marketing features
  • Not for field sales

10. Insightly

Price: $29-$99/user/mo Best for: Project-based businesses (consulting, agencies)

Insightly uniquely combines CRM with project management. When a deal closes, it can automatically convert to a project with tasks, milestones, and deliverables. This makes it ideal for consulting firms, agencies, and professional services.

If your post-sale workflow involves significant project delivery, Insightly eliminates the handoff gap between sales and delivery teams that plagues other CRMs.

Pros

  • CRM + project management unified
  • Deal-to-project conversion
  • Good Google Workspace integration
  • Relationship linking
  • Reasonable pricing

Cons

  • Interface feels dated
  • Marketing automation limited
  • Reporting could be stronger
  • Mobile app needs work

How to Choose the Right Alternative

Rather than comparing features endlessly, focus on these decision criteria:

1. What's Your Primary Pain Point?

  • Too expensive: HubSpot Free, Zoho CRM, or Pipedrive
  • Too complex: Pipedrive or Copper
  • Poor adoption: HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Monday Sales CRM
  • Missing marketing features: HubSpot (clear winner)
  • Need more customization: Microsoft Dynamics 365

2. What's Your Tech Stack?

  • Google Workspace: Copper or HubSpot
  • Microsoft 365: Dynamics 365 or HubSpot
  • Minimal tools: Pipedrive or Freshsales
  • Marketing agency: GoHighLevel

3. What's Your Budget Reality?

  • Under $20/user/month: Pipedrive, Zoho, Monday Sales CRM
  • $20-50/user/month: HubSpot Starter, Copper, Insightly
  • $50-100/user/month: HubSpot Professional, Close, Freshsales
  • Enterprise budget: HubSpot Enterprise, Dynamics 365

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best alternative to Salesforce?
The best Salesforce alternative depends on your needs. HubSpot is best for companies wanting an all-in-one platform with easier setup. Pipedrive is best for sales-focused teams wanting simplicity. Zoho CRM is best for budget-conscious businesses. Microsoft Dynamics 365 is best for enterprises already using Microsoft products. For most small-to-midsize businesses leaving Salesforce, HubSpot offers the closest feature parity with significantly lower complexity.
Why do companies switch from Salesforce?
Companies switch from Salesforce primarily due to cost (total ownership often 2-5x license fees), complexity (requiring dedicated admins), low user adoption (steep learning curve), and over-engineering for their needs. Mid-market companies often find that 80% of Salesforce features go unused, while still paying enterprise prices. Simpler CRMs can deliver better ROI through higher adoption rates.
Is HubSpot really cheaper than Salesforce?
Yes, HubSpot is typically 40-60% cheaper than Salesforce when comparing total cost of ownership. HubSpot includes more features in base pricing, doesn't require dedicated admins, and has faster implementation. A 20-person team on HubSpot Professional costs roughly $21,600/year vs. $39,600+ for Salesforce Enterprise, not including Salesforce's implementation, admin, and add-on costs.
Can small businesses use Salesforce alternatives effectively?
Small businesses often use Salesforce alternatives more effectively than Salesforce itself. Platforms like HubSpot, Pipedrive, and Zoho CRM are designed for smaller teams with limited technical resources. They offer faster setup, lower costs, and higher user adoption rates. Unless you have complex enterprise requirements, a simpler CRM typically delivers better results for small businesses.
What CRM is easiest to migrate to from Salesforce?
HubSpot is generally the easiest to migrate to from Salesforce. HubSpot offers dedicated migration tools, a migration team for larger accounts, and extensive documentation. Most migrations take 2-8 weeks. Zoho CRM and Pipedrive also have straightforward import processes. The key challenge isn't data migration—it's rebuilding automations and retraining users on the new system.
