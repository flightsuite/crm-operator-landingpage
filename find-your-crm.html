Common Questions

What CRM is best for small business?

The best CRM for small business depends on your specific needs. HubSpot CRM offers a robust free tier ideal for startups. GoHighLevel is excellent for marketing agencies and service businesses needing an all-in-one solution. Keap works well for coaches and consultants. Salesforce suits growing teams needing enterprise features. Take our quiz above to get a personalized recommendation.

How much does a CRM cost?

CRM pricing varies widely: HubSpot offers a free CRM with paid tiers from $20-120/month per user. GoHighLevel costs $97-297/month for unlimited users. Salesforce starts at $25/user/month. Keap ranges from $129-199/month. Many CRMs offer free trials. Consider total cost including setup, training, and any implementation help you might need.

Should I use an all-in-one CRM or specialized tools?

All-in-one CRMs like GoHighLevel combine CRM, email marketing, website building, and automation in one platform—ideal for agencies and businesses wanting simplicity. Specialized CRMs excel in specific areas: Salesforce for enterprise sales, Follow Up Boss for real estate, ServiceTitan for home services. Choose all-in-one if you want fewer tools to manage; choose specialized if you need best-in-class features for your industry.

What is GoHighLevel?

GoHighLevel (GHL) is an all-in-one marketing and CRM platform popular with agencies, coaches, and local businesses. It includes CRM, funnel builder, email/SMS marketing, appointment scheduling, website builder, and automation—all in one subscription. Starting at $97/month with unlimited contacts, it's cost-effective for businesses wanting to consolidate their tech stack.

Do I need a CRM consultant to set up my CRM?

It depends on your technical comfort and time. Simple CRMs like HubSpot can be self-setup. Complex platforms like Salesforce or GoHighLevel often benefit from expert help. A CRM consultant can: set up automations correctly, migrate your data, train your team, and avoid costly mistakes.
