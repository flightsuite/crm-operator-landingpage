The 7 Best CRMs for Agencies (Detailed)

1. GoHighLevel

Best for Marketing Agencies
Price: $97-497/mo (flat rate, unlimited users) White-label: Full (Agency Unlimited plan)

GoHighLevel dominates the agency CRM market for good reason. The flat-rate pricing model means you can add unlimited team members and client sub-accounts without increasing costs. The built-in funnel builder, email marketing, SMS, appointment booking, and reputation management replace multiple SaaS subscriptions.

The Agency Unlimited plan ($297/mo) includes full white-labeling‚Äîyour logo, domain, and branding throughout. Many agencies resell GHL access to clients as a recurring revenue stream, creating profit centers beyond service fees.

The trade-off: steeper learning curve than simpler alternatives, and the interface isn't as polished as HubSpot. But for marketing agencies delivering funnels, email campaigns, and automation to clients, nothing else offers this much value at this price point.

Best For

  • Marketing agencies managing multiple SMB clients
  • Agencies wanting to resell CRM/marketing tools
  • Teams needing funnel builders and SMS marketing
  • Agencies prioritizing flat-rate predictable costs

2. HubSpot (Partner Program)

Best for Enterprise Clients
Price: $50-150/user/mo + contact tiers Partner benefits: Commissions, training, certifications

HubSpot becomes the right choice when your clients demand it. Enterprise prospects often prefer recognizable platforms‚Äî"We use HubSpot" carries more weight in board rooms than "We use GoHighLevel." The HubSpot Partner Program offers commissions on referrals, co-marketing opportunities, and certification badges that signal credibility.

For agencies implementing HubSpot for clients (not just using it internally), the Partner Program provides meaningful benefits: training resources, deal registration, and revenue sharing. If your business model includes HubSpot implementation services, the investment makes sense.

The economics only work for agencies targeting enterprise clients who expect HubSpot-tier solutions. For SMB clients, the per-user and per-contact costs quickly become prohibitive.

Best For

  • Agencies serving enterprise/Fortune 500 clients
  • Agencies offering HubSpot implementation services
  • Teams needing recognizable platform credibility
  • Agencies wanting partner program revenue

3. Salesforce

Price: $75-300/user/mo White-label: No

Salesforce is the enterprise default‚Äîwhen Fortune 1000 companies say "CRM," they usually mean Salesforce. For agencies serving these clients, Salesforce expertise becomes a competitive advantage and revenue stream through implementation and customization services.

The Salesforce Partner Program (AppExchange, consulting partner tiers) offers substantial benefits for agencies building practices around the platform. But this requires significant investment: certifications, dedicated admin resources, and deep platform expertise.

For internal agency use (tracking your own sales), Salesforce is almost always overkill. The complexity and cost don't justify the benefits unless you're also selling Salesforce services.

Best For

  • Agencies building Salesforce consulting practices
  • Agencies serving enterprise clients exclusively
  • Teams with dedicated Salesforce admin resources
  • Agencies wanting AppExchange revenue opportunities

4. Monday Sales CRM

Best for Creative Agencies
Price: $12-24/user/mo White-label: No

Monday CRM makes sense for creative and design agencies where projects matter more than marketing automation. The platform seamlessly connects deal tracking with project management‚Äîwhen a deal closes, the project board populates automatically with deliverables and timelines.

For agencies already using Monday.com for project management, adding CRM capabilities keeps everything in one workspace. The visual interface is intuitive for creative teams, and the flexibility allows custom workflows for different client types.

The limitation: no marketing automation, funnel building, or white-labeling. This is purely sales + project management, not a marketing delivery platform like GoHighLevel.

Best For

  • Creative/design agencies with project-based billing
  • Teams already using Monday.com for project management
  • Agencies wanting simple CRM without marketing tools
  • Visual thinkers who prefer boards over lists

5. Pipedrive

Price: $14-99/user/mo White-label: No

Pipedrive is the simplest option for agencies that just need basic deal tracking without client-facing automation features. The visual pipeline is intuitive, adoption rates are high, and the per-deal cost stays predictable regardless of database size.

For agencies where the CRM is purely internal (tracking your own new business pipeline), Pipedrive provides excellent value without the complexity of marketing platforms. It won't help you deliver client work, but it tracks sales efficiently.

Small agencies with straightforward sales processes often find Pipedrive sufficient. You can always graduate to GoHighLevel or HubSpot later as client delivery needs evolve.

Best For

  • Small agencies needing simple deal tracking
  • Teams wanting fast adoption with minimal training
  • Agencies separating CRM from client delivery tools
  • Budget-conscious agencies with basic needs

6. Vendasta

Price: $79-579/mo (flat rate tiers) White-label: Yes (full)

Vendasta is designed specifically for agencies reselling digital marketing services to local businesses. The platform white-labels reputation management, listings, social marketing, and CRM tools that you can resell at margin to clients.

The marketplace model means you can offer services without building everything yourself‚ÄîVendasta fulfills, you sell and support. For local agencies serving restaurants, contractors, and small retailers, this approach scales efficiently.

The trade-off: less flexibility than GoHighLevel for custom funnels and automation, and the marketplace model means you're dependent on Vendasta's fulfillment quality for client satisfaction.

Best For

  • Local agencies serving SMB markets
  • Agencies wanting done-for-you fulfillment options
  • Teams focused on reputation and listings management
  • Agencies building recurring revenue through reselling

7. Teamwork

Price: $10-18/user/mo (CRM + Projects) White-label: No

Teamwork combines CRM with project management, time tracking, and resource planning specifically designed for agencies. The platform understands agency workflows‚Äîretainers, project budgets, utilization rates, and client portals.

The CRM component (Teamwork CRM) integrates natively with Teamwork Projects, creating seamless handoff from closed deal to active project. Time tracking flows into invoicing, utilization dashboards show team capacity, and client portals provide transparency.

For project-based agencies billing hourly or on retainer, Teamwork's integrated approach often beats combining separate CRM and PM tools.

Best For

  • Project-based agencies with hourly billing
  • Teams needing time tracking + CRM integration
  • Agencies wanting client portals for transparency
  • Resource planning and utilization tracking

  • Time tracking - Hours logged against client retainers
  • Resource planning - Team capacity and utilization visibility
  • Client portals - Transparency on project status and deliverables

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best CRM for marketing agencies?
GoHighLevel is the best CRM for marketing agencies in 2026. At $97-497/month flat rate (unlimited clients and users), it offers white-labeling, built-in funnel builders, SMS/email marketing, and appointment booking. For enterprise agencies with larger budgets, HubSpot Partner Edition provides more sophisticated marketing automation and better integrations with enterprise tools.
Can I white-label a CRM for my agency clients?
Yes, several CRMs offer white-labeling for agencies. GoHighLevel ($297/mo Agency Unlimited plan) provides full white-label capabilities‚Äîyour logo, domain, and branding throughout the platform. Vendasta, AgencyAnalytics, and Zoho CRM Plus also offer white-label options, though with varying degrees of customization and different pricing models.
How much should an agency pay for CRM?
Agency CRM costs vary dramatically based on model: Per-user pricing (HubSpot, Salesforce) runs $50-150/user/month, totaling $500-1500/month for a 10-person agency. Flat-rate pricing (GoHighLevel at $97-497/month) is more economical for agencies managing multiple clients. Most agencies find flat-rate models more predictable and cost-effective as they scale.
Should agencies use the same CRM as their clients?
Not necessarily. Many agencies manage client relationships in one CRM (like GoHighLevel or HubSpot) while helping clients use different systems. However, if you offer CRM implementation services, specializing in one platform (becoming a HubSpot Partner or GoHighLevel certified) can be more profitable than spreading expertise across many tools.
Is GoHighLevel worth it for agencies?
GoHighLevel is worth it if you manage 3+ clients and need marketing automation, funnels, or SMS capabilities. At $97/month for unlimited sub-accounts, it replaces multiple tools (CRM + funnel builder + email + SMS + booking). The platform has a steeper learning curve than alternatives, but the economics make sense for agencies serving multiple clients on marketing retainers.
What features do agencies need in a CRM?
Agencies need: multi-client management (sub-accounts or workspaces), project tracking alongside sales pipeline, white-labeling options, proposal and invoice integration, client reporting dashboards, and team collaboration tools. Marketing agencies specifically need email/SMS automation, funnel building, and campaign analytics. Choose based on your service model‚Äîretainer vs project-based.
