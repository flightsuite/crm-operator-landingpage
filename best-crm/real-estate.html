Best CRM for Real Estate Agents in 2026: Complete Guide

January 2026 18 min read
TL;DR

For real estate teams, Follow Up Boss ($69-499/mo) is the industry standard—excellent lead routing, Zillow/Realtor.com integrations, and team accountability features. For solo agents, LionDesk ($25/mo) offers the best value with video messaging and texting built-in. For brokerages wanting IDX + CRM combined, kvCORE provides an all-in-one platform. Don't overpay for brokerage-level tools if you're an individual agent.

Real estate agents face unique CRM challenges. You're juggling leads from multiple sources (Zillow, Realtor.com, Facebook, your website), nurturing prospects through buying cycles that can last 6-18 months, and tracking dozens of property showings and transaction milestones.

Generic CRMs like HubSpot or Pipedrive can work, but you'll spend hours customizing them for real estate workflows. Real estate-specific CRMs come pre-built with the features agents actually need: lead source integrations, drip campaigns for long nurture cycles, showing management, and transaction tracking.

This guide compares the top real estate CRMs based on actual agent usage—not vendor marketing claims. We'll help you find the right tool whether you're a solo agent, team leader, or brokerage owner.

The 8 Best Real Estate CRMs (Detailed Reviews)

1. Follow Up Boss

Best for Teams
Price: $69/mo (Grow), $499/mo (Platform) Best for: Teams wanting lead routing & accountability

Follow Up Boss is the industry standard for real estate teams. Its lead routing engine is unmatched—automatically distribute leads based on round-robin, performance, zip code, lead source, or custom rules. Team leaders get real-time visibility into agent activity and conversion rates.

The 250+ integrations cover virtually every lead source in real estate. Zillow, Realtor.com, Facebook, Google, and dozens of IDX providers connect natively. For teams buying leads from multiple sources, this integration breadth is critical.

Key differentiator: Accountability features let team leaders track call volume, response time, and conversion rates per agent. If your business depends on team performance, this visibility justifies the premium pricing.

Strengths

  • Best-in-class lead routing
  • 250+ native integrations
  • Team accountability dashboard
  • Excellent mobile app
  • Pond system for lead recycling

Limitations

  • Overkill for solo agents
  • No built-in IDX website
  • Higher price than alternatives
  • Learning curve for setup

2. LionDesk

Best for Solo Agents
Price: $25/mo (CRM), $83/mo (CRM Premium) Best for: Individual agents wanting value + video

LionDesk delivers the best value for solo agents. At $25/month, you get features that competitors charge $100+ for: video messaging, texting, power dialer, and drip campaigns. The video email feature alone—sending personalized video messages directly from the CRM—can dramatically increase engagement rates.

Lead capture integrates with major portals (Zillow, Realtor.com), and the transaction management module tracks deals from contract to close. It's not as powerful as Follow Up Boss for teams, but for individual agents, it covers all essential needs at the lowest price point.

Key differentiator: Built-in video messaging at no extra cost. Video emails get 300% higher click rates than text emails—LionDesk makes this easy without third-party tools.

Strengths

  • Lowest price for full features
  • Built-in video messaging
  • Texting included
  • Power dialer available
  • Transaction management

Limitations

  • Less powerful for teams
  • Interface less polished
  • Fewer integrations
  • Support can be slow

3. kvCORE

All-in-One Platform
Price: $299+/mo (varies by brokerage) Best for: Brokerages wanting IDX + CRM unified

kvCORE is an all-in-one platform combining IDX website, lead generation, CRM, and marketing automation. For brokerages that want everything under one roof without stitching together multiple tools, kvCORE eliminates integration headaches.

The platform is particularly strong for team/brokerage rollouts. Agents get individual branded sites that feed into a central CRM. Lead routing, accountability tracking, and enterprise reporting come built-in. Many franchises (KW, RE/MAX) offer kvCORE as part of their tech stack.

Key differentiator: IDX website + CRM + lead gen in one subscription. No need for separate website, CRM, and marketing tools.

Strengths

  • True all-in-one platform
  • IDX website included
  • AI-powered lead scoring
  • Built for brokerage scale
  • Behavioral automation

Limitations

  • Expensive for solo agents
  • Complex setup
  • Can feel bloated
  • Locked into ecosystem

4. Sierra Interactive

Price: $299-499+/mo Best for: Teams focused on lead generation

The CRM integrates tightly with the website—visitor behavior (searches, favorites, time on site) flows into lead profiles for smarter follow-up. Teams using paid advertising appreciate the built-in landing pages and conversion tracking.

The "Success Assurance" program includes dedicated ad management and lead concierge services. For teams spending $10,000+/month on Google and Facebook ads, BoomTown's managed service model can deliver better ROI than self-managed campaigns.

The platform requires more setup time than competitors—you're essentially building your business workflows into the system. But once configured, Realvolve runs your processes automatically.

Can You Use a General CRM for Real Estate?

Yes, but with caveats. General CRMs like HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Zoho CRM can work for real estate if:

  • You're on a tight budget — HubSpot Free gives you unlimited contacts at zero cost
  • You have unique workflow needs — General CRMs offer more customization flexibility
  • You don't need portal integrations — If leads come from your sphere rather than Zillow/Realtor.com
  • You're comfortable with setup time — Expect 10-20 hours customizing for real estate workflows

However, real estate-specific CRMs save significant time. Pre-built Zillow integrations, showing management, and transaction tracking work out of the box. For most agents, the time savings justify the cost difference over a free general CRM.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best CRM for real estate agents?

How much should real estate agents pay for CRM?
Solo agents should budget $25-50/month for a capable real estate CRM. Teams typically spend $69-300/month depending on size and features. Avoid paying for brokerage-level platforms if you're an individual agent—you'll pay for features you can't use. LionDesk ($25/mo) and Follow Up Boss ($69/mo) offer the best value at their respective price points.
Do I need a real estate-specific CRM or can I use HubSpot?
General CRMs like HubSpot can work for real estate, but you'll need to customize heavily for property tracking, showing schedules, and transaction management. Real estate-specific CRMs like Follow Up Boss include these features natively. If you're building a team or want plug-and-play functionality, choose a real estate CRM. Essential features for real estate CRM include: lead capture from Zillow, Realtor.com, and your website; automated drip campaigns for long nurture cycles; showing scheduling and reminders; transaction milestone tracking; mobile app for field access; and integration with your MLS or IDX provider.
