GoHighLevel vs HubSpot 2026: The Complete Comparison for Agencies and Businesses

January 2026 12 min read
TL;DR

GoHighLevel wins for marketing agencies needing white-label capabilities, unlimited sub-accounts, and all-in-one marketing tools at a flat monthly rate. HubSpot wins for in-house marketing teams, enterprises, and businesses prioritizing integrations, support, and established credibility. Choose GoHighLevel if you run an agency serving multiple clients. Choose HubSpot if you're managing marketing for a single business with complex needs.

GoHighLevel (GHL) and HubSpot represent two fundamentally different approaches to CRM and marketing automation. HubSpot pioneered the inbound marketing category and serves everyone from startups to enterprises. GoHighLevel emerged specifically for agencies—designed to be white-labeled and resold to clients at scale.

This isn't a simple "which is better" comparison. These platforms serve different audiences with different business models. Understanding that distinction is crucial to making the right choice.

We've integrated FlightSuite with both platforms, so we understand their strengths and limitations firsthand. Here's an honest comparison based on real usage, not marketing claims.

Pricing: The Most Dramatic Difference

Pricing is where GoHighLevel and HubSpot differ most dramatically. It's also where most agencies make their decision.

GoHighLevel Pricing (2026)

  • Agency Starter: $97/month - Single account, unlimited contacts, core features
  • Agency Unlimited: $297/month - Unlimited sub-accounts, white-labeling, API access
  • Agency Pro (SaaS Mode): $497/month - Rebilling, advanced white-label, priority support

What's included: CRM, email marketing, SMS marketing, funnel builder, appointment scheduling, reputation management, website builder, membership sites, and more. No per-user or per-contact fees.

HubSpot Pricing (2026)

  • Free CRM: $0 - Basic features, limited automation
  • Starter Bundle: $30/month - Core marketing, sales, service tools
  • Professional Marketing Hub: $800/month - Advanced automation, reporting
  • Enterprise Marketing Hub: $3,600/month - Full enterprise features

Additional costs to consider:

  • Contact tiers: Pricing increases as your database grows
  • Additional users: $15-$150/user/month depending on tier
  • Add-ons: SMS, additional reporting, API calls
  • Onboarding: Required for Professional ($3,000+) and Enterprise ($6,000+)

Pricing Verdict

For an agency managing 50 clients, GoHighLevel costs $297/month flat. The equivalent HubSpot setup would require 50 separate accounts, easily exceeding $15,000/month. For agencies, GoHighLevel's pricing is genuinely disruptive. For single businesses, the comparison is closer, especially at HubSpot's lower tiers.

The Agency Use Case: Where GoHighLevel Dominates

GoHighLevel was built specifically for the agency business model. Here's what that means in practice:

White-Label Everything

With GoHighLevel's Agency Unlimited plan, you can completely rebrand the platform. Your clients see your logo, your colors, your domain. They never know GoHighLevel exists. You become the software company.

HubSpot doesn't offer white-labeling. When you use HubSpot for clients, they know they're using HubSpot—and they could leave your agency and keep using HubSpot directly.

Unlimited Sub-Accounts

In GoHighLevel, each client gets their own "sub-account" with isolated data, separate workflows, and unique settings. You can manage 5 clients or 500 clients without pricing changes on the Unlimited plan.

With HubSpot, each client would need their own HubSpot account, each with its own subscription cost. At scale, this becomes prohibitively expensive.

SaaS Mode: Build Your Own Software Company

GoHighLevel's Pro plan ($497/month) enables "SaaS Mode"—you can charge your clients monthly subscriptions for access to your white-labeled platform. GoHighLevel handles billing, and you keep the margins. Many agencies generate $10,000-$50,000/month in SaaS revenue this way.

The Enterprise Use Case: Where HubSpot Dominates

For businesses managing their own marketing (not agency clients), HubSpot offers significant advantages:

Integration Ecosystem

HubSpot connects natively with over 1,500 applications. Salesforce, Shopify, WordPress, Zoom, Slack, QuickBooks—if it's a business tool, HubSpot probably integrates with it. This ecosystem is mature and reliable.

GoHighLevel has around 200 integrations, growing but still limited. Complex enterprise tech stacks may require workarounds or custom development.

Enterprise Credibility

When presenting to C-suite executives or procurement teams, HubSpot is a known quantity. It's publicly traded, SEC-compliant, and trusted by Fortune 500 companies. This matters for enterprise sales cycles and vendor approval processes.

GoHighLevel is newer, privately held, and less recognized outside the agency world. For some enterprise deals, this could be a barrier.

Support and Documentation

HubSpot's support infrastructure is extensive: phone support, dedicated customer success managers (at higher tiers), HubSpot Academy certifications, and comprehensive documentation. When something breaks, you have clear escalation paths.

GoHighLevel relies more on chat support and community forums (particularly the active Facebook group). For critical business operations, this can be limiting.

Feature Deep Dive: Key Comparisons

Sales Funnels & Landing Pages

GoHighLevel excels here. Its funnel builder rivals ClickFunnels, with drag-and-drop editing, A/B testing, order forms, and membership site creation. Agencies can build sophisticated multi-step funnels quickly.

HubSpot offers landing pages, but they're more traditional. You won't find the same sales-funnel-specific features like order bumps, upsells, or countdown timers without third-party tools.

Winner: GoHighLevel, by a significant margin.

Email Marketing

GoHighLevel has solid email marketing with automation, templates, and deliverability that's improved significantly. However, it lacks some advanced features like A/B testing subject lines at scale.

HubSpot offers mature email marketing with smart sending times, A/B testing, personalization tokens, and excellent deliverability. The email builder is more polished.

Winner: HubSpot, though GoHighLevel is adequate for most use cases.

SMS Marketing

GoHighLevel has SMS built into its core. Two-way texting, drip campaigns, appointment reminders, and review requests via text are all native features. SMS costs are pay-as-you-go on top of the platform fee.

HubSpot has limited SMS capabilities. You'll need third-party integrations (like Sakari or Twilio) for robust SMS marketing.

Winner: GoHighLevel, decisively.

CRM & Pipeline Management

Both platforms offer solid CRM functionality with customizable pipelines, deal tracking, and contact management. HubSpot's CRM is more polished and has better automation triggers. GoHighLevel's is functional but less refined.

Winner: HubSpot, though both are capable.

Who Should Choose GoHighLevel?

GoHighLevel is the better choice if you:

  • Run a marketing agency serving multiple clients
  • Want to white-label and resell software
  • Need SMS marketing as a core channel
  • Build sales funnels for clients
  • Want predictable, flat-rate pricing
  • Don't need extensive third-party integrations
  • Are comfortable with community-based support
  • Want to build recurring SaaS revenue

Who Should Choose HubSpot?

HubSpot is the better choice if you:

  • Manage marketing for your own business (not clients)
  • Need enterprise credibility for sales cycles
  • Require extensive integrations with existing tools
  • Value polished UI and comprehensive support
  • Need advanced reporting and attribution
  • Want strong blog/SEO tools built-in
  • Have budget for per-user/per-contact pricing
  • Need HubSpot Academy certifications for team training

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GoHighLevel better than HubSpot?
GoHighLevel is better than HubSpot for marketing agencies that need white-label capabilities, unlimited client sub-accounts, and aggressive pricing. HubSpot is better for in-house marketing teams, enterprises, and businesses that need robust integrations, advanced reporting, and established brand credibility. The best choice depends on whether you're an agency serving clients or a business managing your own marketing.
How much does GoHighLevel cost compared to HubSpot?
GoHighLevel starts at $97/month for unlimited contacts with the Agency Starter plan, or $297/month for the Agency Unlimited plan with white-labeling. HubSpot starts free but paid plans range from $15/user/month to $3,600/month for enterprise features. For agencies with multiple clients, GoHighLevel is dramatically cheaper—often 70-90% less than equivalent HubSpot functionality.
Can GoHighLevel replace HubSpot?
GoHighLevel can replace HubSpot for most agency and small business use cases, particularly for sales funnels, appointment booking, SMS marketing, and client management. However, HubSpot has stronger blog/SEO tools, more native integrations, better enterprise features, and superior customer support. Agencies often prefer GoHighLevel; in-house teams often prefer HubSpot.
Which CRM is better for agencies, GoHighLevel or HubSpot?
GoHighLevel is significantly better for agencies due to its unlimited sub-accounts, white-label capabilities, and flat-rate pricing regardless of client count. Agencies can rebrand GoHighLevel as their own platform and manage hundreds of clients without per-seat costs. HubSpot's per-contact and per-seat pricing makes it prohibitively expensive for agency use cases.
Does GoHighLevel have good customer support?
GoHighLevel's customer support is adequate but not as comprehensive as HubSpot's. GHL offers chat support, a knowledge base, and an active Facebook community. However, wait times can be longer, and complex issues sometimes require community help. HubSpot offers phone support, dedicated success managers (on higher tiers), and more extensive documentation. For critical business operations, HubSpot's support is more reliable.
Can I migrate from HubSpot to GoHighLevel?
Yes, you can migrate from HubSpot to GoHighLevel, though it requires manual effort. GoHighLevel offers CSV import for contacts and basic data. However, you'll need to rebuild automations, funnels, and workflows from scratch. Expect 2-4 weeks for a complete migration. Many agencies maintain both platforms during transition to ensure no data loss or service interruption.
