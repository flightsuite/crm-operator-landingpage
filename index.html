Why FlightSuite?

Questions sales teams ask

What is FlightSuite?

FlightSuite is the universal CRM CoPilot that makes CRM adoption effortless. Instead of logging into your dashboard and clicking around, you type what happened in natural language—like "Just called John, he wants the enterprise plan"—and FlightSuite automatically logs it to GoHighLevel or HubSpot. The average user saves 5-10 hours per week.

How does CRM automation work?

FlightSuite uses AI to understand natural language and map it to your CRM fields automatically. When you type "Met Sarah at conference, she needs pricing by Friday," the AI identifies the contact, logs the meeting note, and can create a follow-up task—all without you clicking through CRM forms.

How much time does CRM data entry take?

The average user spends 5-10 hours per week logging into CRM dashboards, clicking through forms, and entering data manually—that's 20% of their productive time. FlightSuite makes CRM adoption effortless by eliminating this busywork entirely, letting your team focus on closing deals instead of updating records.

What CRMs does FlightSuite support?

FlightSuite currently integrates with GoHighLevel and HubSpot, with both integrations fully live. Salesforce support is coming soon—join the waitlist to be first in line. We're also planning Zoho CRM support for 2026. The extension works directly in Chrome alongside your CRM.

Is my CRM data secure with FlightSuite?

FlightSuite prioritizes your data security. We only access CRM data when you're actively using the extension, never store your CRM credentials on our servers, and never share data with third parties. You maintain full control, and your CRM data stays in your CRM—we just help you put it there faster.

How do I start using FlightSuite?

Getting started takes under two minutes. Install the free Chrome extension from the Web Store, connect your CRM account (GoHighLevel or HubSpot), and start typing naturally. Most users make their first automated CRM update within 10 minutes. No training required—if you can type, you can use FlightSuite.

Installation Guide

1

Install Extension

Click the button below to install FlightSuite from the Chrome Web Store.

Open Chrome Web Store →
2

Get Your Location ID

Find your GoHighLevel Location ID:

  • Log in to your GoHighLevel account
  • Go to Settings > Business Profile
  • Copy your Location ID
3

Create Private Integration Token

Generate a Private Integration Token in GoHighLevel:

  • Navigate to Settings > Private Integrations
  • Click "Create New Token"
  • Enter a name (e.g., "FlightSuite Integration")
  • Select all permissions for full CRM automation
  • Confirm the security warning when prompted
  • Copy and save your Private App Key immediately

⚠️ Save your token securely—it won't be shown again.

4

Enter Credentials

In the FlightSuite extension:

  • Click the FlightSuite icon in Chrome
  • Paste your Location ID
  • Paste your Private Integration Token
  • Click "Connect" and start using FlightSuite!
2

Navigate to Legacy Apps

Create a Legacy App in HubSpot:

  • Log in to your HubSpot account
  • Click the Settings gear icon
  • Go to Integrations > Legacy Apps
  • Click "Create Legacy App"
  • Select "Private" as the app type
3

Configure Scopes & Get Token

Set up permissions and copy your token:

  • Name your app (e.g., "FlightSuite Integration")
  • Click the Scopes tab
  • Enable read/write access for Contacts, Companies, Deals
  • Click "Create App" then "Continue"
  • Click the Auth tab and copy your Access Token

📋 See the full guide for the complete list of 18 required scopes.

4

Enter Credentials

In the FlightSuite extension:

  • Click the FlightSuite icon in Chrome
  • Select "HubSpot" as your CRM
  • Paste your Access Token
  • Click "Connect" and start using FlightSuite!

What's a CRM?

Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

A CRM is software that helps businesses manage their customer relationships, track sales activities, and organize contact information. Think of it as a central hub for all your customer interactions, sales pipeline, and business data.

Who needs a CRM?

Sales Teams tracking leads and closing deals
Small Businesses managing customer relationships
Service Providers organizing client communications
Marketing Teams tracking campaign engagement
